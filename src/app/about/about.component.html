ABOUT

    A farmer (also called an agriculturer) is a person engaged in agriculture,
raising living organisms for food or raw materials.The term usually applies to people who do some
combination of raising field crops, orchards, vineyards, poultry, or other livestock. A farmer might
own the farmed land or might work as a laborer on land owned by others, but in advanced economies,
a farmer is usually a farm owner, while employees of the farm are known as farm workers, or farmhands.
However, in the not so distant past, a farmer was a person who promotes or improves the growth of
(a plant, crop, etc.) by labor and attention, land or crops or raises animals (as livestock or fish).



    Over 1/2 billion farmers are smallholders, most of whom are in developing
countries, and who economically support almost two billion people. Globally, women constitute more than 40%
of agricultural employees.
